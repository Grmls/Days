Filipino most requested "Lutong Bahay"

Lutong Bahay A website dedicated in introducing different dishes in the Filipino Cuisine to the world. We feature various recipes like meals and snacks, which you can cook right at your own home. In fact, the term “lutong-bahay or lutong bahay” translates to “Home cooked”, and the site aims to help you with that. Filipino, Asian or not, you’re welcome to browse through the site for the best and popular Filipino dishes. Get the recipes you want, so you and your companions could have a great time on the table.

ADOBO 		Adobo refers to a method of marinating and stewing
for any cut of meat or fish in a briny mixture
of vinegar, soy sauce, and spices.		 Philippines Meat (beef, chicken, pork), seafood, or vegetables;
soy sauce, vinegar, cooking oil, garlic, black
peppercorn, bay leaf
KALDERETA 		Kaldereta or caldereta is a goat meat stew from the
Philippines. Variations of the dish use beef, chicken,
or pork. Commonly, the goat meat is stewed with vegetables and
liver paste. Vegetables may include tomatoes,
potatoes, olives, bell peppers, and hot peppers.		 Philippines Beef, pork, chicken, Tomato sauce, Liver spread, garlic,
bay leaves, sugar, liver spread, green and red bell
pepper, potatoes, carrot, and pickles
MENUDO 		Menudo, also known as ginamay or ginagmay (Cebuano:
"[chopped into] smaller pieces"), is a traditional stew from
the Philippines made with pork and sliced liver in tomato
sauce with carrots and potatoes.		 Philippines Oil, potatoes, carrot, garlic, onion, pork, liver,
tomato sauce, water, sugar, pepper, raisins, and bell pepper
TINOLANG MANOK 		Tinola is a traditional Filipino comfort food that usually
consists of broth, chicken, leafy greens, papaya, and ginger.		 Philippines broth, chicken, leafy greens, papaya, and ginger.
DINUGUAN 		Dinuguan (Tagalog pronunciation: [dɪnʊgʊˈʔan]) is a Filipino
savory stew usually of pork offal (typically lungs, kidneys, intestines,
ears, heart and snout) and/or meat simmered in a rich, spicy
dark gravy of pig blood, garlic, chili (most often siling haba),
and vinegar.		 Philippines lungs, kidneys, intestines, ears, heart and snout and/or meat
simmered in a rich, spicy dark gravy of pig blood, garlic, chili (most
often siling haba), and vinegar.
SISIG 		Sisig (/ˈsiːsɪɡ/ Tagalog pronunciation: ['sisig]) is a Filipino
dish made from pork jowl and ears (maskara), pork belly, and chicken
liver, which is usually seasoned with calamansi, onions, and
chili peppers. It originates from the Pampanga region in Luzon.		 Pamppanga, Philippines pork jowl and ears (maskara), pork belly, and chicken liver,
calamansi, onions, and chili peppers.
TORTANG TALONG 		Tortang talong (or torta) is a Filipino eggplant omelette made
by pan-frying a charred eggplant doused in (vegan) egg. The outside of
the omelette is golden brown and crispy, while the core remains
soft and creamy. This dish is a popular breakfast or lunch in the Philippines
and requires just a few simple ingredients		 Philippines Egg plant, oil, salt, pepper, egg
